THE TERRIBLE LAST SENTENCE:
“THE WAVES BROKE ON THE SHORE.”

TWO MEANINGS ARE POSSIBLE:

1) A PESSIMISTIC ONE: HERE, WOOLF IS NOT SAYING THAT THE WAVES FLOW
AND FLOW BACK, COME AND GO, NOT AT ALL, SHE USES THE VERB: TO
BREAK. AND TO BREAK IS NOT TO MOVE. IN THIS INTERPRETATION, DEATH
WINS AND BERNARD WAS LOST IN PURE DELUSION WHEN HE BELIEVED THAT
HE WAS “UNVANQUISHED AND UNYIELDING”’.

2) BUT YOU CAN ALSO ARGUE THAT THE BREAK IS A MOMENTARY
INTERRUPTION, THE PROOF THAT DEATH AND SEPARATION ARE REAL.
HOWEVER, IT DOES MEAN THAT DEATH AND SEPARATION HAVE THE LAST
WORD: THE BREAK, THE RUPTURE, SEPARATION AND DEATH, ARE JUST LOCAL
AND MOMENTARY EVENTS IN THE ETERNAL RENEWAL OF LIFE. THE SECOND
INTERPRETATION IS MORE OPTIMISTIC THAT THE FIRST ONE; IT DOES NOT MEAN
THAT THIS INTERPRETATION IS THE TRUEST. 29



--- Page 31 ---
GENERAL CONCLUSION

WE ARE MORTAL BEINGS AND WE FEAR DEATH. WOOLF INCORPORATES THIS
IMPASSABLE TRUTH INTO A COSMIC FRAMEWORK: THE BIRTH AND THE DEATH OF
HUMAN BEINGS ARE JUST THE DRAMATIZATION OF SOMETHING THAT ALSO
HAPPENS TO NATURAL ENTITIES, LIKE THE SUN AND THE OCEAN.

FOR WOOLF, EACH HUMAN BEING SUFFERS FROM BEING SEPARATED FROM THE
OTHERS; BUT THIS SEPARATION IS NOT ABSOLUTE: WE RETAIN IN OURSELVES
SOMETHING COMING FROM THE OTHER PERSONS: IN US, THERE IS SOMETHING
THAT IS MORE THAN US.

THE MONOLOGUES ARE NOT THE EXPRESSIONS OF COMPLETELY SINGULARIZED
CHARACTERS, THEY REVEAL THE PART OF US THAT IS NOT SINGULARIZED, THIS
PART OF US THAT HAS NO FACE. THANKS TO THIS UNFINISHED PART, WE CAN
BECOME WHO AND WHAT WE ARE NOT, WE CAN DEEPLY COMMUNICATE WITH
THE OTHER BEINGS. IN THIS RESPECT, TO BE INCOMPLETE IS A CHANCE: AS WE
ARE UNFINISHED, POTENTIAL BEINGS, WE CAN BEAR AN INFINITY OF MASKS, WE
CAN FEEL, THANKS TO OUR “SYMPATHETIC IMAGINATION” (COETZEE), WHAT
NON-HUMANS FEEL.
