WHAT IS A MANIFESTO ¢

A MANIFESTO IS A SPECIAL KIND OF
PUBLIC DECLARATION WHOSE GOAL IS TO

PROMOTE A RADICAL SHIFT IN POLITICS
OR ARTS.

--- Page 15 ---
SURREALISM

“SURREALISM, N. PSYCHIC AUTOMATISM IN
ITS PURE STATE, BY WHICH ONE PROPOSES TO
EXPRESS -- VERBALLY, BY MEANS OF THE
WRITTEN WORD, OR IN ANY OTHER MANNER --
THE ACTUAL FUNCTIONING OF THOUGHT.
DICTATED BY THE THOUGHT, IN THE ABSENCE
OF ANY CONTROL EXERCISED BY REASON,
EXEMPT FROM ANY AESTHETIC OR MORAL
CONCERN” (BRETON, 26)

THE SURREALIST MANIFESTO OF
1924 WANTED TO BRING WAR IN
LITERATURE, TO PUT POETRY ON

IN THIS REGARD, THE FIRST

SURREALIST MANIFESTO WAS ALSO
A POLITICAL ACT, CLEARLY
DEFINING THE ENEMY (REALISM)
AND CALLING FOR A RADICAL
SHIFT IN HUMAN EXISTENCE.

ALLEN GINSBERG, “HUM BOM!”

composed of short words, short sentences - a
sort of caricature of the grotesque language
used in official discourses: a way to expose the
gross simplicity of war discourse: “we” against
“them”.

A metaphorical bomb, not a real one: able
to wake us up from our apathy. Ginsberg
wants to use his poem as a tool able to
arm politics.
