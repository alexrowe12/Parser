“WEIRD STORIES” (1)

“...THE ILLUSION OF SOME STRANGE SUSPENSION OR
VIOLATION OF THE GALLING LIMITATIONS OF TIME, SPACE,
AND NATURAL LAW WHICH FOR EVER IMPRISON US AND
FRUSTRATE OUR CURIOSITY ABOUT THE INFINITE COSMIC
SPACES BEYOND THE RADIUS OF OUR SIGHT AND ANALYSIS.
[WEIRD] STORIES FREQUENTLY EMPHASISE THE ELEMENT OF
HORROR BECAUSE FEAR IS OUR DEEPEST AND STRONGEST

CREATE A CONVINCING PICTURE OF SHATTERED NATURAL
LAW OR COSMIC ALIENAGE OR “OUTSIDENESS” WITHOUT
LAYING STRESS ON THE EMOTION OF FEAR.”

CONCLUSION

¢ 1) IF WE FOLLOW WHAT THE STORY TELLS US, THE MEANING OF THE
NARRATIVE IS THAT, ONE DAY, CTHULHU WILL CALL AND THE GREAT
OLD ONES WILL RULE THE WORLD;

2) BUT IF WE PAY ATTENTION TO THE TITLE ITSELF, IF WE PROVIDE A
META-NARRATIVE INTERPRETATION, WE SHOULD ADD THAT THE WHOLE
STORY IS ABOUT THE DIFFICULTY TO CALL, I.E. TO NAME THE HIDDEN
THINGS. MAYBE THE CALL OF CTHULHU IS THE CALL OF OUR
UNCONSCIOUS. MAYBE CTHULHU IS JUST A GENERAL NAME FOR AIL
THE THINGS THAT WE REFUSE TO SEE, TO HEAR, AND TO
ACKNOWLEDGE: ONE DAY, IN THE PAST, THESE THINGS, THESE IDEAS,
THESE FEELINGS WERE PRESENT, MANIFEST; BUT NOW, THEY ARE
REPRESSED, BURIED UNDER THE SEA OF OUR CONSCIOUSNESS.
