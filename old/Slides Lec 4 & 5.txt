CONCLUSION OF THIS PART OF THE LECTURE

Apocalypse Now Is a translation of Heart of
Darkness insofar as the film is able to express the
darkness, the anxiety and the madness of the book.

But the film has lost the distance that Conrad sets
Up with the frame story artifice: in Coppola’s film,
the apocalypse happens now, without the void of
the unnamed voice, without mediation.

WHAT IS THE DARKNESS OF HEART OF DARKNESS ¢?

1) IT SEEMS TO BE THE DARKNESS OF AFRICA = THE SO-CALLED “DARK
CONTINENT.” DARK MEANS A) BLACK, BUT B) FIRST AND FOREMOST THE
UNKNOWN, FOR THE COLONISTS: THE DARKNESS OF HEART OF DARKNESS
REFERS TO A WESTERN UNDERSTANDING OF AFRICA AS A PLACE OF
DARKNESS AND UNKNOWABILITY;

2) DARKNESS CAN BE LOCATED EVERYWHERE: “AND THIS [BY WHICH HE
MEANS ENGLAND] ALSO HAS BEEN ONE OF THE DARK PLACES OF THE
EARTH” (P.5) = ENGLAND BEFORE THE ROMAN INVASION. DARKNESS IS
THE SITUATION PRECEDING EVERY KIND OF IMPERIALIST AND COLONIALIST
ACTIONS;

3) DARKNESS = THE BRUTALITY OF IMPERIALISM AND COLONIALISM.
KURTZ’S BRUTALITY IS THE HIDDEN TRUTH OF IMPERIALISM;

4) BUT IT’S ALSO THE TRUTH OF THE DEATH DRIVE THAT FREUD DISCOVERED
AS THE HIDDEN REALITY OF EVERY HUMAN BEING AND EVERY SOCIETY.
